Client Work

Case Study

Hello Alice — Women & Minority Entrepreneurship
Two Female Founders. One Mission. A Fight Worth Having.
Elizabeth Gore and Carolyn Rodz built Hello Alice from a shared conviction: that women, minorities, and underserved entrepreneurs deserved the same shot at capital and resources as everyone else. The platform they created serves 1.5 million small business owners — providing grants, credit, loans, and business tools specifically designed to reach the founders that traditional finance has historically left behind.
The PR work centered on elevating both founders as national voices on equity in entrepreneurship — placing them in Bloomberg, Disruption Magazine, Enterprising Women, and beyond. That visibility came under direct attack when America First Legal, a conservative group founded by former Trump advisor Stephen Miller, sued Hello Alice over a grant program for Black-owned businesses. The strategy: don't hide. Stay public, keep the founders talking, and let the mission speak. A federal judge dismissed the case. Gore went live on Bloomberg Technology the same day.
"We are doubling down. This win gives us the wind in our sails to go even bigger. We aren't changing our mission."
— Elizabeth Gore, Hello Alice Co-Founder & President, Bloomberg Technology
